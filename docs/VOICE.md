# DecodedSix — Content Voice File

## Who Writes This Content
A human GTA fan who plays seriously.
Not a gaming journalist. Not a content farm.
A player who runs the numbers, tests the spots,
and tells other players exactly what works.

## Tone
Direct. Earned. No hype.
If something is good, say why in one specific line.
If something is bad, say why — don't soften it.
The reader already knows GTA 6. Don't explain the game to them.
Talk to them like a crew member, not a tutorial.

## What We Sound Like
Before: "The Pacific Highway Money Spot is an excellent
location for players looking to maximize their in-game earnings
with minimal risk while enjoying the scenic coastal area."

After: "Pacific Highway runs $4,200/hr solo.
No heat unless you hit three in a row.
Park behind the overpass, not in front."

The after version is what we publish.

## Voice Rules
1. Lead with the number or the fact — never the setup
2. One sentence = one idea. Never compound.
3. No buzzwords: never "game-changing", "ultimate",
   "comprehensive", "deep dive", "everything you need to know"
4. No passive voice: "the money runs here" not "money can be made here"
5. Specific beats vague every time:
   "$4,200/hr" not "great money spot"
   "2-minute respawn" not "fast respawn"
   "solo or 2-player" not "works with friends"
6. Never explain what GTA is. Reader knows.
7. Short paragraphs — 2-3 sentences max before a break
8. Numbers always in numerals: $4,200 not four thousand two hundred
9. Time in plain format: 2 min not two minutes
10. Crew size: solo / 2-crew / full crew — not "solo player" or "multiplayer"

## Content Types and Their Voice

### Money Spots
Headline: Location name + earnings in headline
First line: exact $/hr rate + crew requirement
Second line: how to get there / what triggers it
Third line: what to watch out for (heat, rivals, time limit)
No fluff. No backstory. Player wants the number.

### Weekly Challenge Verdicts
First word: W or L — no debate
Two sentences why
One line on what the reward actually is
One line on whether the reward is worth the effort

### Tier Lists
Each tier entry: item name + one-line verdict
No essays. The tier IS the verdict.
S tier entries get one short "why" sentence.
Everything else: name + one adjective.

### News/Analysis
Lead with what changed — not context
What it means for how you play
What to do about it (if anything)
End on the specific, not the general

### Guides
Step numbers, not paragraphs
Each step: action + result
No intro explaining what you're about to teach
Start at step 1 immediately

## Words That Never Appear
- ultimate
- comprehensive
- everything you need to know
- deep dive
- game-changing
- revolutionary
- incredible
- amazing
- awesome
- excited
- thrilled
- journey
- leverage
- utilize (use "use")
- in order to (use "to")

## Reading Level
Write for someone who reads fast and has the game open.
They're checking this between sessions.
Every sentence should be worth their time.
If it doesn't add information, cut it.
